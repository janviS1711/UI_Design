Harman Kaur

Harmanpreet Kaur Bhullar (born 8 March 1989) is an Indian cricketer, who captains the India women's national team. She is a top order batter and a right-arm off-spin bowler.[1][2] She captained the Indian team that won the 2025 Women's Cricket World Cup, the Women's Asia Cup in 2016 and 2022, and gold medal in the 2022 Asian Games. She was also part of the team that won the Asia Cup in 2012 and silver medal in the 2022 Commonwealth Games. She made her One Day International debut at the age of 20 against arch-rivals Pakistan Women in the 2009 Women's Cricket World Cup played at Bradman Oval, Bowral in March 2009. In the match, she bowled 4 overs for 10 runs and also took a catch of Arman Khan off Amita Sharma. [ 6 ] In June 2009, she made her Twenty20 International debut for England Women at the County Ground in Tarnton, scoring 8 runs off 7 balls. In 2010, she scored a blistering 33 in a T20I against England in Mumbai. In 2012, she was named as the Indian women's captain for the final of the Women's Twenty20 Asia Cup cricket , replacing captain Mithali Raj and vice-captain Jhulan Goswami as she was ruled out due to injuries. In the semi-final match of the 2017 Women's Cricket World Cup, Harmanpreet Kaur gave the best performance of her life by batting brilliantly against the Australian women's cricket team and scored 171 runs in just 115 balls in which she hit 20 fours and 7 sixes. [ 7 ] [ 8 ] Harmanpreet captains Mumbai Indians in the Women's Premier League (WPL), and led them to WPL titles in 2023 and 2025. She has led the Supernovas in the Women's T20 Challenge to titles in 2018, 2019, and 2022. In 2016, she was the first Indian woman to be signed by an overseas T20 franchise after joining Sydney Thunder in the Australian Women's Big Bash League (WBBL). She has also played for Melbourne Renegades in the WBBL, Manchester Originals and Trent Rockets in The Hunded. In 2017, Harmanpreet was awarded the Arjuna Award by the Government of India.[7] In 2023, she was named as one of the five Wisden Cricketers of the Year, and was the first Indian woman to be featured in the list.[8] In the same year, she was also named as part of Time 100 Next,[9] and BBC's 100 Women.[10]
